Bruschetta

A classic Italian appetizer that's simple, fresh, and bursting with the flavors of tomato, garlic, and basil.

Ingredients:

  • 1 baguette, sliced into 1-inch rounds
  • 2 cups cherry tomatoes, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (plus 1 whole clove, halved)
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 190¬∞C (375¬∞F). Toast baguette slices on a baking sheet until golden brown.
  2. Lightly rub the surface of the toasted bread with the halved garlic clove for extra flavor.
  3. In a bowl, mix diced tomatoes, minced garlic, basil, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  4. Spoon the mixture generously onto each toasted slice.
  5. Serve immediately for the best texture and flavor.
Pro-Tip: For an extra touch of elegance, drizzle a high-quality balsamic glaze over the top just before serving.

Stuffed Mushrooms

A savory and satisfying appetizer, these mushrooms are filled with a creamy, cheesy, and garlicky mixture.

Ingredients:

  • 12 large button or cremini mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tbsp breadcrumbs (optional, for topping)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 180¬∞C (350¬∞F). Lightly grease a baking sheet.
  2. Carefully remove stems from mushrooms. Finely chop the stems.
  3. In a bowl, combine chopped stems, cream cheese, Parmesan, garlic, and parsley. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Stuff each mushroom cap generously with the mixture. Sprinkle breadcrumbs on top if desired.
  5. Place on the baking tray and bake for 18‚Äì20 minutes until the mushrooms are tender and the filling is golden.
  6. Serve warm and enjoy!

Caprese Skewers

A beautiful, no-cook appetizer that showcases the fresh flavors of Italy. Perfect for a quick and easy party snack.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 1 container (8 oz) fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini)
  • 1 bunch fresh basil leaves
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tbsp balsamic glaze
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Small wooden skewers

Steps:

  1. On each skewer, thread one cherry tomato, one basil leaf (folded), and one mozzarella ball.
  2. Repeat the pattern until the skewer is full, leaving a little space at each end.
  3. Arrange the finished skewers on a serving platter.
  4. Just before serving, drizzle with olive oil and balsamic glaze.
  5. Season lightly with salt and freshly ground black pepper.
Pro-Tip: To intensify the flavor, you can marinate the mozzarella balls in olive oil, garlic, and herbs for 30 minutes before assembling.
